Code of Conduct

To maintain a warm, welcoming, and safe environment in the Forge Nest community, all participants are expected to adhere to the following code of conduct.

Rules

  1. Be kind and courteous to your fellow members.
  2. Criticize ideas, not people.
  3. Discuss publicly and refrain from direct messaging people unless specifically requested.
  4. Don’t be offensive.
  5. Ask questions; don’t ask to ask. There’s no need to write, “Is there anyone that can help me with X?” ask about X.

Prohibitions

  1. No violence or threats of violence of any kind.
  2. No sexist, racist, homophobic, transphobic, or other prejudiced or discriminatory language or actions; this includes jokes.
  3. No sexual harassment, including unwelcome sexual advances or offensive remarks about a person’s sex.
  4. No cursing or swearing at other people (but feel free to use curses and swears about the #$%@ code, IDE, language, hardware, or physics).
  5. No NSFW material. It includes nudity, gore, or any other potentially disturbing imagery.
  6. No revealing another’s private information against their wishes (doxing).
  7. No stalking or intimidation, online or otherwise.
  8. No spamming, trolling, flaming, or other disruptions of the peace.
  9. No advocating or threatening any of the above behaviors.

Where this Code of Conduct applies

This code of conduct pertains to our online and but not limited to offline community platforms.

Consequences for violations

If a participant engages in behavior violating this code of conduct, the core members of this community may take any action they deem appropriate. Actions may range from a simple warning to the most drastic: expulsion from the community, exclusion from any interaction, and loss of all rights in the community.

For reporting violations, you can reach out to the community team by emailing forgenest.community@gmail.com

Additions, Amendments, and Feedback

This code of conduct is subject to updates and improvements. As online culture evolves and new sensitivities emerge, additions may be necessary. Community members are encouraged to provide feedback for continuous improvement.
